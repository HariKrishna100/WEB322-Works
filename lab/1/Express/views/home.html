Maasai Mara National Game Reserve

Maasai Mara was established in 1961 by Narok County Government has an area of 580 square miles (1,510km2) it is a large national game reserve located in Narok, Kenya which borders with the Serengeti National Park in Tanzania. It was named after the ancestral inhabitants of the Maasai people who migrated to the area and they live day to day with the nature.

Maasai Mara has 6 entry exit gates which open at 6:00am an close at 6:00pm after that period there's no permission to enter or exit the reserve and all the game drives must end before 6pm in Maasai Mara. If you're planning for night Game drives you will have opportunity to watch nocturnal animals (animals most active at night than daytime) such as hippos, lions, leopards, porcupines, civet cats, aardvarks, cape hares and white-tailed mongoose. Night game drives occur at certain private Mara Conservancies, and they are conducted by an authorised guide and a driver. The drive starts at 7pm and must end before 9pm. For the best view the vehicles used for night drives have powerful lights for clear visibility nor they are harmful to animals.

The Big Five

Maasai Mara has diverse group of animals and the most famous are the Big Five category which consists of:
  1. Lion
  2. Elephant
  3. Rhino
  4. Leopard
  5. Buffalo
